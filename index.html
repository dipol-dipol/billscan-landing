The Problem

Medical billing is broken. You're paying the price.

Hospitals and insurers use billing codes that no regular person can understand. Mistakes happen constantly, and they almost always favor the provider.

Upcoding. Duplicate charges. Unbundled procedures. These aren't rare glitches. They're standard operating procedure in an industry where complexity benefits the biller.

Most people pay whatever the bill says because disputing feels impossible. You'd need to understand CPT codes, know the correct pricing, and write a formal dispute letter. Who has time for that?

That's exactly why these errors persist. The system counts on you giving up.

FAQ

Common questions

How accurate is BillScan?

BillScan uses pattern matching and rate comparison against industry databases to flag likely errors. It's not a replacement for a professional medical billing advocate, but it catches the most common issues that cost people money. We show you exactly why each item was flagged so you can make your own judgment.

What types of errors can BillScan detect?

Duplicate charges, upcoding (billing for a more expensive procedure than performed), unbundling (charging separately for items that should be grouped), mismatched EOB amounts, and charges for services not documented in your records.

Will disputing actually work?

It depends on the error. Clear-cut mistakes like duplicate charges are usually corrected quickly. Upcoding disputes may require more back-and-forth. Either way, BillScan gives you a legitimate, documented starting point. Many billing departments resolve disputes simply because someone took the time to ask.

Is my data safe?

Yes. All uploads are encrypted with AES-256. We do not sell or share your data with any third party. You can delete your documents immediately after receiving your report, and we offer automatic deletion for users who prefer it.

How is this different from Rocket Money or GoodRx?

Rocket Money tracks subscriptions and requires access to your bank account. GoodRx helps you find cheaper prescriptions. BillScan audits medical bills you've already received. We don't need your bank login, and we focus specifically on finding billing errors, not cutting costs elsewhere.
